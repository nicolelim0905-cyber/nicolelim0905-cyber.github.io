Power BI • Python • DAX • Excel | 2025

Executive Summary

Developed comprehensive analytics solution for a leading construction machinery manufacturer to optimize production scheduling and inventory management. Built interactive Power BI dashboards to identify bottlenecks, reduce delays, and improve operational efficiency across multiple warehouses and product lines.

🎯 Business Understanding

⚠️ Business Problem

Inaccurate demand forecasting and poor production planning are leading to frequent last-minute changes, production delays, and inventory imbalances. This causes higher costs, inefficiencies, and challenges in fulfilling dealer and customer orders on time.

🎯 Objectives

Maximize operational efficiency and responsiveness in production scheduling.

⚙️ Constraints

Must minimize stockouts and inventory imbalance to ensure timely fulfillment.

🤝 Business Success Criteria

Achieve at least 15% reduction in production lead time.

💰 Economic Success Criteria

10% reduction in inventory holding cost.

⚙️ Technical Implementation & DAX Calculations

Key DAX Measures & Business Logic

Lead Time Performance Tracking:
LeadTimeReductionPct = DIVIDE(
  [LeadTime_Baseline] - [LeadTime_Current],
  [LeadTime_Baseline])

Inventory Cost Optimization:
InventoryCostReductionPct = DIVIDE(
  [InventoryCost_2021] - [InventoryCost_Current],
  [InventoryCost_2021])

Goal Achievement Indicators:
InventoryGoalMet = IF([InventoryCostReductionPct] >= 0.10,
  "✔ Goal Met", "❌ Below Target")
LeadTimeGoalMet = IF([LeadTimeReductionPct] >= 0.15,
  "✔ Goal Met", "❌ Below Target")

Technical Implementation Details

Baseline Establishment:
  • LeadTime_Baseline: Q1 2021 average as performance benchmark
  • InventoryCost_2021: 2021 full-year baseline for cost comparison
Performance Calculations:
  • Cost Factor: Applied standard multiplier of 5 for inventory holding cost
  • Period Filtering: Used CALCULATE with year/quarter filters for baseline comparison
  • Visual Indicators: Implemented checkmarks (✔) and cross marks (❌) for goal status
Time Intelligence:
  • MonthYear: FORMAT([Date], "MMM YYYY") for readable date display
  • YearMonthSort: FORMAT([Date], "YYYYMM") for proper chronological sorting

Data Processing & Feature Engineering

Dataset Cleaning: Reduced from 6,814 to 4,828 rows after handling nulls and duplicates
Missing Data: Applied data imputation (Change_Type = 'Unknown') and removed ~5% missing records
Outlier Treatment: Applied IQR winsorization on Order_Quantity, Inventory_Level, Lead_Time_Days, Delay_Days
Feature Engineering: Created temporal features (Year, Quarter, Month) and business categories

Advanced Data Processing:
  • Normalization: Used MinMaxScaler for consistent visual comparison across metrics
  • Categorization: Discretized Delay_Days into On-Time/Moderate/Severe categories
  • Business Logic: Created Order_Size_Category (Small/Medium/Large) based on quantity thresholds
  • Time Intelligence: Parsed and aligned Month-Year values for proper Power BI sorting

Optimizing Steel Rod Offcut Utilization
Python • Excel • Statistical Analysis • EDA | 2025

Executive Summary

Conducted comprehensive exploratory data analysis for a leading steel pipe manufacturer to minimize production waste and maximize material utilization. Built a data-driven system using EDA to identify waste patterns and developed strategic recommendations to reduce scrap losses and improve order fulfillment using leftover inventory.

🎯 Business Understanding

⚠️ Business Problem

Underutilized steel rod offcuts are sold as scrap, leading to revenue loss and material wastage.

High Level Solution: Build a data-driven system using EDA to identify waste patterns and potential optimization.

🎯 Objectives

  • Maximize utilization of steel rods
  • Reduce material wastage by reusing inventory

⚙️ Constraints

  • Current inventory usage relies on manual calculations
  • Limited visibility into real-time offcut inventory

🤝 Business Success Criteria

  • Increase offcut utilization by at least 15%
  • Improve order fulfillment using leftover inventory

💰 Economic Success Criteria

Achieve cost savings of at least $1M through reduced scrap.

📊 Data Understanding & Processing

Dataset Characteristics

Volume: ~33,000 rows of transaction-level rod order data
Source: Excel-based TMT rod inventory and sales records
Key Variables: Length, Dia, Rate, Quantity, Type, Date
Initial Findings: Highly skewed quantity distribution, many custom cuts ("CAB"), small orders (<2MT)

Preprocessing Techniques

  • Missing Value Treatment: Mean imputation for Quantity and Rate columns
  • Outlier Management: IQR-based winsorization for extreme values
  • Data Normalization: Min-Max scaling for Quantity and Rate
  • Feature Engineering: Created Is_Custom, Waste_Risk, and Potential_Loss flags
  • Discretization: Binned Quantity into Low/High categories

Processing Challenges

  • Mixed Data Types: Handling varied "Length" field formats
  • Custom Cut Rules: Identifying CAB and 10-12m format patterns
  • Data Quality: Inconsistent entries like "0 METER" requiring validation
  • Statistical Distribution: Mean ~5.9 MT, Skewness +2.2, Kurtosis ~10

📊 Data Analysis Visualizations

Effect of Data Preprocessing
Purpose: Before/after comparison showing winsorization impact, distribution normalization, and outlier treatment effectiveness
Business Insights & Waste Analysis
Purpose: Waste risk identification, financial loss patterns, product type analysis, and scrap loss concentration by length

Critical Business Insights

  • Waste Risk Correlation: Custom cuts and small orders (<2 MT) highly correlated with material wastage
  • Custom Length Impact: CAB orders and non-standard lengths (7-10M) show low reuse potential
  • Financial Loss Patterns: Small, expensive orders create leftover pieces leading to higher losses
  • 12M Rod Problem: 12M and customized lengths contribute disproportionately to scrap losses
  • High-Value Risk: Low correlation between quantity and rate means high-value losses occur on small orders

Operational Gaps Identified

  • Manual Planning Issues: Mismatches between order requirements and available offcuts
  • Tracking Inconsistencies: Inconsistent order format entries (e.g., "0 METER")
  • Standardization Gaps: Lack of standardization in order tracking and inventory management
  • Real-time Visibility: Limited visibility into current offcut inventory status
  • Risk Identification: No automated system for flagging high-waste-risk orders

Strategic Improvement Plan

  • Standard Length Promotion: Encourage standard lengths where possible to improve reusability
  • Ordering Rules Redesign: Implement minimum order requirements (e.g., 2MT for custom cuts)
  • Dynamic Pricing Strategy: Increase rates for risky orders, offer discounts for standard sizes
  • Offcut Matching System: Match existing offcuts to future demand requirements
  • Inventory Tracking: Store and track reusable pieces efficiently with automated flags

📋 Project Impact & Business Value

Data-Driven Transformation: Created business logic flags (Is_Custom, Waste_Risk, Potential_Loss) that enable automated identification of high-risk orders. These insights provide immediate actionable intelligence for inventory planning, pricing strategy, and order management optimization.

Strategic Value: The comprehensive EDA revealed that waste-risk orders are heavily concentrated in small-quantity custom orders, with 12M rods and customized lengths driving disproportionate financial losses. Implementation of the 5-point improvement strategy enables better inventory planning, minimizes scrap, and unlocks immediate cost savings through data-driven decision making.
